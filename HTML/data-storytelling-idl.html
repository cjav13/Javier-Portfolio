Data-Storytelling - Interdisciplinary Learning

This interdisciplinary project involves students from Big Data & Analytics and Pharmaceutical Science working collaboratively to ensure Liteo tablets meet British Pharmacopoeia standards and to provide data-driven decisions to stakeholders if any issues are identified in the data. Using manufacturing data, we are required to analyze trends, assess data quality, and build a storytelling dashboard, applying domain knowledge from both big data and pharmaceutical science.

Domain Understanding

Our team begins by sharing domain knowledge from Big Data & Analytics and Pharmaceutical Science to build a shared understanding of the background, manufacturing process, and dataset. This includes discussing pharmaceutical requirements like uniformity of weight and key data concepts such as data quality checks, visualisation, and storytelling to align the team before analysis begins.

Data Management

Next, we check for data issues such as missing values and inconsistencies, and perform cleaning where necessary. This is followed by domain-informed transformations, including calculations based on British Pharmacopoeia requirements, such as percentage deviation, along with other useful transformations to support analysis. The dataset is then reformatted through pivoting and integrated into a central source for analysis.

Data Visualizations & Analytical Insights

Visualizations are created to uncover trends, patterns, correlations, and potential issues in the manufacturing data. These visuals help support or rule out hypotheses, guide further investigation, and communicate findings clearly. Dashboards are also developed to present key metrics and insights in an interactive and accessible format for stakeholders.

Data Storytelling & Recommendations

Lastly, all charts and dashboards are brought together to form a data story that highlights key findings. Based on these insights, actionable recommendations are provided to stakeholders to support decision-making and improve the tablet manufacturing process.
